Road Trip from Tangier: Discovering the Best of Northern Morocco:

Northern Morocco is a treasure trove of diverse landscapes, rich history, and vibrant cultures. Starting your journey from Tangier, a road trip through this region offers a perfect blend of coastal beauty, mountain vistas, and historic towns. Here's a guide to discovering the best of Northern Morocco on a road trip from Tangier.

Day 1: Tangier to Asilah

Distance: 46 km Travel Time: 1 hour Start your road trip by heading southwest along the coast to Asilah, a charming seaside town known for its whitewashed buildings and colorful murals.

Day 2: Asilah to Chefchaouen

Distance: 113 km Travel Time: 2.5 hours From Asilah, drive southeast to Chefchaouen, the famous Blue City nestled in the Rif Mountains.

Day 3: Chefchaouen to Tetouan

Distance: 68 km Travel Time: 1.5 hours Continue your journey to Tetouan, a city with a strong Andalusian influence and a UNESCO World Heritage medina.

Day 4: Tetouan to M’Diq

Distance: 30 km Travel Time: 30 minutes Drive to the coastal town of M’Diq, known for its marina and beautiful beaches.

Day 5: M’Diq to Fnideq and Ceuta

Distance: 21 km Travel Time: 30 minutes Head further along the coast to Fnideq and the Spanish enclave of Ceuta, a unique blend of cultures.

Day 6: Fnideq to Larache

Distance: 120 km Travel Time: 2 hours Travel south along the coast to Larache, a town with a rich history and beautiful river views.

Day 7: Larache to Tangier via Cap Spartel

Distance: 84 km Travel Time: 1.5 hours On your return to Tangier, make a detour to Cap Spartel for some final scenic views.

Tips for a Successful Road Trip Plan Your Route: Use a GPS or a detailed map to plan your journey and ensure you don’t miss any key attractions. Pack Essentials: Bring water, snacks, a first aid kit, and a fully charged phone with a car charger. Stay Safe: Follow local traffic laws, drive defensively, and be prepared for varying road conditions. Book Accommodations: Reserve your accommodations in advance, especially during peak travel seasons. Local Currency: Have some local currency on hand for tolls, parking, and small purchases.

Final Thoughts A road trip from Tangier through Northern Morocco offers a unique opportunity to experience the region’s diverse landscapes and rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re exploring vibrant medinas, relaxing on pristine beaches, or hiking in the Rif Mountains, this journey promises unforgettable memories and breathtaking sights. Enjoy the freedom of the open road and the endless discoveries that await you in Northern Morocco.
